Leaf beetles, also known as leaf beetles, are the Chrysomelidae family of beetles containing more than 35,000 species organized in more than 2,500 genera, forming one of the largest and most common of all beetles families. Several subfamilies are recognized, but the exact classification and systematics are likely to change with continued research.
